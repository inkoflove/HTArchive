About Us

HyperText Archives is maintained by the Special Provision for Recovered Documents (SPRD), an independent non-governmental organization dedicated to the preservation and public documentation of historically significant classified materials. We operate under the belief that information of historical value should remain accessible to future generations. According to the SPDD Charter, documents considered abandoned, forgotten, or improperly withheld from public knowledge are independently acquired, archived, analyzed, and republished for research purposes. The articles published on this website are based on materials allegedly recovered from numerous government agencies, military archives, research institutions, and other confidential repositories. These documents are declassified through unauthorized archival recovery procedures and rewritten into publicly accessible research articles by SPRD analysts. All documents are preserved in Xenocode, the organization's archival writing protocol. Xenocode intentionally combines multiple human languages within a single document in order to avoid detection by Government operated AI spying and censorship tools. The SPRD neither confirms nor denies the authenticity of any recovered document. Readers are encouraged to conduct their own independent analysis and draw their own conclusions.
